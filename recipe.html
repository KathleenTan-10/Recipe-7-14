The Great Depression Water Pie

The Origin of Delectable Water Pie
The Great Depression Water Pie is a simple and affordable dessert that was popular during the Great Depression era in the United States. It is made with basic ingredients such as water, sugar, flour, and butter, which were readily available and inexpensive at the time. The pie has a custard-like filling that is light and sweet, making it a comforting treat for families during difficult times. This period began in 1929 and lasted until the late 1930s, a time where many people faced economic hardship and sought simple pleasures in their daily lives. Many families relied on such simple recipes to make the most of what they had available. Although it was famously known to be called as the "Great Depression Water Pie", it actually existed before mass unemployment and financial crisis. A recipe was actually found to lead back to 1908, and another that traces back to the 1880s. In 1908, the pie was published by Mrs. Hollis Crews as a submission to a cooking competition located in Fulton, Missouri called the Fulton Gazette. We will be using the 1908 recipe as our main reference.
Ingredients:
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 tbsp flour
  • 5 tbsp butter
  • unbaked, 1 (9-inch) empty pie crust (mixing flour, water, salt/sugar/lard )
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

How to Make It:
  1. Preheat oven to 400°F and set empty pie crust on a baking sheet.
  2. Pour 1½ cups water into the pie crust.
  3. In a small bowl, stir together flour and sugar. Then, sprinkle it evenly over water in crust. Don't stir.
  4. Next, add the vanilla over the water that's in the crust. Place pats of butter on top of this. Don't mash the butter and leave it as a small chunk.
  5. Bake at 400°F for 30 minutes. Reduce heat to 375°F and cover sides of crust if needed to prevent burning. Continue cooking for an additional 30 minutes.
  6. In the end, let the pie cool so that way it hardens. The mixture will start of watery, but if you let it sit for a while, and after place it in the fridge, you will eventually get a consistancy you'll enjoy.
